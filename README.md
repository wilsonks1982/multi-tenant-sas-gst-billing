# GST Billing SaaS — Business Plan

## Executive Summary

We are building a cloud-based GST billing and business administration platform for Indian small and medium businesses.

Our goal is to help businesses manage:
- GST-compliant billing
- tenant and company administration
- user access and role control
- subscription billing
- audit visibility
- operational dashboards

The product is designed as a multi-tenant SaaS application, with each tenant representing a customer workspace and each company representing a business entity under that tenant.

This is not positioned as a full ERP. It is intended to be a focused, modern, India-first billing and operational platform for businesses that need something stronger than basic invoice tools but simpler than heavyweight enterprise systems.

In the early stage, the company should be run with discipline:
- tight product scope
- strong customer feedback loop
- modest burn
- recurring subscription revenue
- clear roadmap from bootstrap to angel-backed growth

---

## Problem Statement

Many Indian businesses still manage billing with a mix of:
- spreadsheets
- desktop software
- disconnected accounting workflows
- manual admin processes

There are several recurring problems:

### 1. Billing tools are often too basic
Many products can generate invoices, but do not provide:
- strong admin workflows
- user/company access control
- auditability
- structured SaaS-style business operations

### 2. Larger systems are often too complex
ERP and enterprise accounting solutions can be:
- expensive
- difficult to implement
- too broad for small teams
- not intuitive for founder-led or finance-led operations

### 3. GST-compliant operations still create friction
Indian businesses need software that understands:
- GSTIN-based workflows
- tax-aware invoicing
- company-level identity
- practical operational use cases in India

### 4. Operational visibility is weak
Small businesses often lack:
- centralized user control
- clear audit trails
- business dashboards
- subscription and payment visibility

## Our Solution

We are building a multi-tenant billing platform focused on practical business operations in India.

### Core product scope
The first version of the product focuses on:

- tenant onboarding
- company creation and management
- user onboarding
- company-based user access
- admin dashboards
- audit logs
- SaaS subscription billing visibility
- billing and operational reporting

### Product philosophy
The product should be:
- simple enough for smaller teams
- structured enough for serious business use
- secure and auditable
- designed around Indian billing realities

## Target Market

### Primary customers
Our initial target customers are:

- GST-registered small businesses
- service firms
- local distributors
- small manufacturers
- agencies
- multi-company operators
- growing businesses with simple internal admin needs

### Ideal customer profile in the early stage
The ideal early customer is likely to be:

- a business with 5 to 50 employees
- one or more companies/entities
- regular need for invoice generation
- sensitivity to compliance and process clarity
- currently using inefficient manual or semi-manual workflows

### Early adopters
The best early adopters are customers who already feel pain in:
- billing consistency
- access management
- admin confusion
- business process visibility

These customers are more likely to pay for workflow improvement than customers looking only for the cheapest invoice generator.

---

## Market Opportunity

India has a large base of GST-registered businesses and a growing willingness to adopt cloud software.

This creates a meaningful long-term opportunity, but the near-term approach should be realistic:

- we do not need to win the entire market early
- we only need to win a specific segment first
- we should focus on a narrow wedge and expand from there

The opportunity is not just invoice generation. The larger opportunity is becoming the operational billing layer for small and growing Indian businesses.

---

## Business Model

This will be a recurring revenue SaaS business.

### Primary revenue stream: subscriptions
We expect the main revenue to come from monthly and annual plans.

Example pricing direction:

- **Starter** — ₹1,499 to ₹2,499 per month
- **Growth** — ₹3,999 to ₹4,999 per month
- **Business** — ₹6,999 to ₹7,999 per month
- **Enterprise** — custom pricing

Exact pricing should be validated with real customers before finalizing.

### Secondary revenue streams
Over time, we can add:
- annual contracts
- extra user seats
- premium reporting
- advanced admin controls
- migration/onboarding services
- API or integration access
- accountant-friendly add-ons

### Revenue philosophy
Early revenue should come from:
- customers with real operational pain
- modest but sustainable pricing
- high trust and strong support
- keeping churn low

At this stage, credibility and retention matter more than maximizing top-line pricing.

---

## Why the Revenue Model Can Work

A practical billing SaaS can become a strong business if it does three things well:

### 1. Solves a recurring problem
Billing and compliance are not occasional needs. They are recurring operational needs.

### 2. Becomes part of daily workflow
Once a team uses a system for billing, users, and admin control, switching becomes harder.

### 3. Expands naturally with the customer
As a business grows, it may need:
- more users
- more companies
- more invoices
- better controls
- higher plans

That creates a reasonable path to expansion revenue.

## Go-To-Market Strategy

In the beginning, go-to-market should be focused and low-cost.

### Initial distribution channels
- founder-led demos
- direct outreach
- referrals
- accountant / consultant relationships
- local SME networks
- product demos to friendly early adopters

### Why this is realistic
Early-stage B2B SaaS usually sells through trust, iteration, and direct conversations — not large-scale paid marketing.

### What we should validate first
- who buys fastest
- who activates fastest
- who churns least
- which feature set drives willingness to pay

### Likely early GTM motion
1. onboard a few design partners
2. refine workflow based on real usage
3. close first paying customers manually
4. improve onboarding
5. standardize pricing and demos
6. gradually improve repeatability

## Pricing Strategy

Pricing should be simple and confidence-building.

### Early pricing principles
- easy to understand
- no overly complicated tiers
- enough margin to support the product
- low friction for early customers
- annual discount for cash flow

### Suggested early approach
- one or two paid tiers initially
- offer annual discount
- avoid overengineering pricing too early
- revise based on customer interviews and usage patterns

At an early stage, pricing simplicity is usually more valuable than pricing sophistication.

## Risks and Constraints

### 1. Market crowding
Billing and accounting are competitive categories.

**Response:** stay focused on a specific operational segment and deliver a better workflow experience.

### 2. Product sprawl
There is a temptation to become a full accounting suite too early.

**Response:** remain focused on billing, admin, access, and operational clarity.

### 3. Slow trust-building
Businesses are cautious with billing tools.

**Response:** prioritize reliability, auditability, support, and simple onboarding.

### 4. Price sensitivity
Some SMEs are extremely price-sensitive.

**Response:** target customers with real pain and measurable workflow value, not only bargain seekers.
